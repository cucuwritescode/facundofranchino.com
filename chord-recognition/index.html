Template-based Chord Recognition Introduction

by Facundo Franchino

Introduction

Assuming a piece of music is given in the form of an audio recording, an interesting problem to solve in hopes of analysing harmony is that of automatic chord recognition. To achieve this, one should split up the recording into segments and find an efficient and reliable method to assign a chord label to each generated segment.

Two-Stage Approach

Such systems are typically two-staged:

First, we must cut the recording into frames, and each frame thus be transformed into a feature vector. As most ACR systems, we'll bank on chroma-based audio features, which provide a compact, octave-invariant representation of the harmonic content by aggregating energy into twelve pitch classes.

Secondly, we utilise pattern matching to compare each feature vector against a dictionary of predefined chord templates (hence template-based ACR). These are prototypical vectors representing, for example, 24 major and minor triads. By calculating a similarity measure for each template, we assign the chord that maximises this similarity score for the given frame/segment.
